BIG NEWS

Bryce Harper

Arguably the best free agent of the last 18 years has a new team. Bryce Harper has agreed to a free agent contract with the Philadelphia Phillies, the team announced Friday night. Harper's deal is for 13 years and $330 million and features no opt-out clauses, according to ESPN's Jeff Passan. The deal is the largest in MLB history, breaking Giancarlo Stanton's previous record $325 million contract he signed with the Marlins in November of 2014.

LA Lakers

After signing LeBron James over the summer, it seemed like there was good reason for the Los Angeles Lakers to be heavy favorites in Las Vegas to make the playoffs. However, due to multiple injuries in the season, the LA Lakers have a long way to go. The Lakers will need to find a new coach after firing Luke Walton. In addition to a new coach, the will also need a new head of basketball operations before the NBA draft and 2019 free agency starts. It looks to be like a busy summer for the Lakers.
